What is the Lectionary?

A lectionary, according to Wikipedia, is "a book or listing that contains a collection of scripture readings appointed for Christian or Judaic worship on a given day or occasion." Essentially, it's a calendar for when to read certain scriptures, like a year-round reading plan.

Many of these lectionaries have been put together over centuries; among Christian circles, a handful of widely-used lectionaries remain. The most widely-used and modified lectionary is the Roman Catholic Mass Lectionary, which provides readings for Sunday Mass as well as Daily Mass during the week - this site provides the RCML reading for the current day, with references pulled from the EWTN daily readings and scripture pulled from Bible-API.

What do the colors mean?

The primary background reflects the color of the current liturgical season: purple for Advent & Lent, gold for Christmas & Easter, red for Holy Week & Pentecost, and green for Ordinary Time.

The color of the title also reflects the current liturgical season, with, red also signifying a feast day (also signalled by a subtitle below the date in the sidebar.)
