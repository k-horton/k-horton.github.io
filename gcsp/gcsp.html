Grand Challenge Scholars Program | An Overview

The Grand Challenge Scholars Program (GCSP) is an initiative for students in K-12 and engineering schools to engage with the National Academy of Engineering's (NAE) 14 "grand challenges," or goals that the NAE recognized as necessary to deliver their vision, “Continuation of life on the planet, making our world more sustainable, secure, healthy, and joyful.”

As a student in the GCSP, I'm doing work towards two of these challenges that fulfills the key competencies of the program: academic ("talent"), multicultural, entrepreneurial, multidisciplinary, and socially conscientious. For more details about my research and the different competencies that have gone into it, check out the links in the sidebar; and if you want to learn a little bit more about who I am, keep scrolling!


About Me

Hey there! My name is Kimberly, and I'm a senior Computer Science major at George Fox University.

The first thing you should know about me is that I like to keep myself busy; over the course of my high school years, I’ve been on my school’s parliamentary debate team, an actress in multiple school productions, a soundboard runner at my church, and taught myself tuba, guitar, saxophone, and euphonium. In university, I’ve been in Drumline, Pep Band, George Fox’s nationally-recognized Symphonic Band (playing the euphonium & tuba), multiple choirs, and am a student in the university’s George Fox Honors Program, a Socratic seminar-style Great Books program.

My home is international. I’m an alumnus of Dalat International School in Penang, Malaysia, where I attended middle school and high school; and for the first three months of 2020, I lived in Amman, Jordan as a student in the CCCU's Middle East Studies Program.

Mission Statement

I am a Christian, student, and programmer in Portland, Oregon with homes around the world in Malaysia, Jordan, and across the United States.

I am a lifelong follower of Christ, and I believe his call to serve the poor and underprivileged was meant to be taken seriously. I also believe there is great need in the humanitarian sector for technical experience to come alongside existing efforts in communication and fieldwork; I seek to use my technical experience to bring efficiency and new solutions to those already on the front lines fighting for justice and human flourishing.

Technology is a powerful tool, and I believe it should be harnessed and stewarded responsibly to the benefit of the world at large. I hope to be a part of the movements working for justice and against suffering by learning to steward technology rightly and using it to advance quality of life around the world.
